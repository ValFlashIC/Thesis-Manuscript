\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:optim:conclu}

\lettrine{T}{his} chapter explored optimizations and extensions to \acrlong{spdc}, focusing on enhancing its performance and scalability for sparse matrix computations.
We refined the \acrshort{rtl}-level implementation to maximize efficiency and easy-of-use, ensuring \acrlong{spdc} remains robust and adaptable to modern computational demands.

We extended \acrlong{spdc} to matrix-matrix multiplication kernels, focusing on \acrshort{spmspm}, demonstrating its versatility beyond \acrshort{spmv}.
This extension highlights its adaptability to complex operations, reinforcing its potential as an important component for diverse sparse matrix computations.

A key focus was integrating \acrlong{spdc} into multi-level, multi-core architectures, addressing the need for scalable, distributed processing.
By demonstrating potential extension to parallel systems, \acrlong{spdc} becomes a viable solution for large-scale sparse kernels.

While this chapter presents conceptual insights and proposed optimizations, no evaluation has been conducted yet.
These proposals position \acrlong{spdc} as a versatile and scalable solution for modern sparse matrix processing systems.
