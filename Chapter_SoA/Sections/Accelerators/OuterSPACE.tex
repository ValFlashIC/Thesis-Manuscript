\subsection{OuterSPACE}
\label{sec:soa:outerspace}

OuterSPACE~\cite{Pal2018_OuterSPACE} is an \acrshort{op} accelerator with a focus on \acrshort{spmspm} kernels, although it can also handle \acrshort{spmv}.
It is a highly parallelized architecture which employs \acrfull{spmd}-style \acrfullpl{pe} that fetch data from main memory through a two-level highly-banked cache hierarchy as shown in Figure~\ref{fig:soa:outerspace}.
The input matrices $A$ and $B$ are stored in \acrshort{csc} and \acrshort{csr} formats, respectively, to match the read access patterns of the \acrshort{op} algorithm.
The \acrfullpl{po} and final output matrix $C$ are stored in \acrshort{csc} format.

The computation is temporally divided into the \textit{multiply} and \textit{merge} phases, as shown in Figure~\ref{fig:soa:outerspaceHow}.
During the first phase, inputs are divided into \glspl{tile} of several rows of $A$ and columns of $B$ and distributed through the \acrfullpl{pt} by a \textit{control unit}.
Within each \acrshort{pt}, each \acrshort{pe} processes multiplications of one element of the $k$\textsuperscript{th} column of $A$ with all elements of the $k$\textsuperscript{th} row of $B$ and an L0 cache in the \acrshort{pt} facilitates the reuse of the $B$-rows between \acrshortpl{pe}.
This phase generates a \acrshort{po} per \acrshort{pt}, corresponding to the \gls{tile} of rows of $A$ and columns of $B$ that it was assigned.

Then, during the \textit{merge} phase, the \acrshortpl{po} are combined to generate the final matrix $C$.
Each \acrshort{pt} locally sorts the \acrshortpl{po}, and then the \acrshortpl{pt} work together to merge them.
This algorithm, which is a tiled, \textit{deferred updates} version of \acrshort{op}, was chosen to maximize data locality and parallelism, although it is less efficient.
This \textit{merge} phase only uses half of the \acrshortpl{pe} so the others are disabled (clock-gated) and the caches are reconfigured in a scratchpad mode to save energy.

Note, if non-zero elements in the \acrshortpl{po} exceeds the L0 cache capacity, it overflows to the last-level cache, which results in reduced performance.
OuterSPACE performs best when the $\mathit{nnz}_c(A)$ and $\mathit{nnz}_r(B)$ are both uniform which ensures a uniform distribution of work across the large number of \acrshortpl{pt} and \acrshortpl{pe}.

\begin{highlight}{Conclusion}{Red}
    \textbf{OuterSPACE} is a highly parallelized, \acrshort{op}-based accelerator for \acrshort{spmspm}, leveraging a two-level cache hierarchy and \acrshort{spmd}-style \acrshortpl{pe} to optimize data locality and parallelism through tiled, \textit{deferred update} computation.
\end{highlight}
